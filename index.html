The Problem

Calving is critical – and too often, calves and cows suffer preventable losses

Manual monitoring is exhausting and ineffective, especially at night when most calvings occur.

Current Approaches: Exhausting & Ineffective

Traditional methods require exhausting 24/7 manual monitoring with round-the-clock staffing, leading to farmer burnout

Nighttime monitoring is particularly challenging yet crucial, as most calvings occur during these hours

Manual monitoring diverts valuable labor resources from other essential farm operations, reducing overall productivity

Existing detection systems lack reliability and precision, often missing critical calving events or giving false alarms

Sensor-based systems are physically invasive for animals, require a device per cow, and are prone to loss or damage

Other available solutions lock farmers into expensive proprietary ecosystems with high maintenance costs

Real-World Validation

Pilot Program Progress

10+ Farms Surveyed

Comprehensive research across diverse farming operations confirms that producers are actively seeking reliable, non-invasive calving monitoring solutions that reduce labor costs while improving outcomes.

Confirmed Market Demand

Multiple agricultural operations have expressed strong interest in implementing CalvAlert technology, validating our market research and confirming the significant need for innovative monitoring solutions in the livestock sector.

Pilot Testing Underway

Our first pilot program is currently active on a partner farm in Cyprus, with additional installations scheduled in the Netherlands to ensure CalvAlert is tested across diverse farming environments and operational contexts.

Moving Toward Full Deployment

We're actively refining the CalvAlert system based on real-world implementation feedback, enhancing both hardware and software components to ensure optimal performance before advancing to full commercial deployment.
